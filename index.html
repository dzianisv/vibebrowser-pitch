Why In-Browser vs. Cloud-Hosted Agents?

Cloud agents (like OpenAI's browser agent) face critical limitations:

  • Geo/IP blocking: Cloud IPs are blocked. Residential IPs required for real-world sites.
  • Limited context: No access to user's browsing history, saved credentials, or sessions.
  • CAPTCHA & anti-bot: Cloud browsers get flagged and blocked by anti-bot systems.
  • Modern auth: Passkey authentication requires local device—impossible in cloud.
  • Privacy: User data stays local, not sent to cloud infrastructure.

Why Vibe AI Browser Co-Pilot?

  • While competitors like Comet collect user data, we prioritize privacy above all. This means:
    - Our AI agent integrates with Gemini Nano, embedded directly in Google Chrome. No external LLMs required—ensuring absolute privacy and offline functionality. ℹ️
    - We're developing self-hosted LLMs (Llama, Qwen, Mistral) on TEE machines/Confidential computing. This provides open-source backend code, giving customers cryptographic proof that our agent operates with APIs that neither log, store, nor sell data. ℹ️
    - No single agent excels at complex tasks. We've tested OpenAI Agent, Perplexity Comet, Composite, and BrowserOS—there's ample room for innovation and competition.
  • DOM-based vs. Screenshot: We support two modes: Indexed DOM Nodes + Vision. Vision enhances quality but isn't mandatory. Without vision models, our AI copilot seamlessly works with the local Gemini Nano embedded in Google Chrome.
  • Stealth-mode origins: Why build when Comet and Composite exist? We started development before their announcements and identified critical gaps in their offerings.
  • LLM provider freedom: Choose your preferred LLM and provider—OpenAI, Gemini, Anthropic, OpenRouter, or locally hosted options for maximum privacy. We're ready for enterprise integrations prioritizing data protection.
